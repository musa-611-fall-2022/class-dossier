My Experiences

Professionally, and academically, I have always been a designer. From 2015 to 2019, I went to the Rhode Island School of Design in Providence, RI (lovely city!) where I studied Industrial Design. Although physical craft was the primary course of study in my department, I tended to gravitate towards the digital - my prototypes were shoddy while my slide decks sparkled.

On either side of my senior year, I completed fellowships in planning / planning-adjacent city departments: In 2018 as a Communication Design Fellow with the Providence Department of Planning and Development, and in 2019 as a Summer Fellow in the Boston Mayor's Office of New Urban Mechanics. It was in the booming brutalist halls of Boston City Hall that I fell in love with all things civic, and decided to eventually pursue a graduate degree to find my way back to some city hall somewhere.

Unfortunately, a certain global event put my plans on hold, and I paid the bills in the meantime through freelance graphic and web design. Coasting off a month-long web design course taken at RISD, I mainly stayed on the surface-level of sites - branding, accessibility, building and customizing Squarespace and Wordpress sites. All important work, but I itched to get into the "meat" of that work - doing the research and analysis, building the tools, and guiding the course of work. Cue a re-opening of the world, a slew of graduate school applications, and my finding my new home in Philadelphia and the start of a new chapter of my life!

My Interests
