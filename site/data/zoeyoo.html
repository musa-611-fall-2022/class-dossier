My Experiences

For my undergraduate education, I attended the University of California, Berkeley between the years of 2016 and 2020. I majored in Sustainable Environmental Design, within the College of Environmental Design, with minors in GIS and Urban Planning. Though I originally meant to study with more of a focus on environmental studies, I found my passions lay in geospatial analysis and urban planning; in particular, transportation planning and advocacy. So, I decided to attend graduate school for Urban Planning, studying Sustainable Transportation and Infrastructure Planning (STIP). As I only took one year between my undergraduate and graduate programs, I have not had significant work experience in planning. At UC Berkeley, I worked for two years at the Museum of Vertebrate Zoology, mapping the geographic extents of amphibian species; this included frogs, salamanders, and caecilians (little wormlike creatures). I loved learning about these vibrant creatures and exploring the Bay Area with friends to find what reptiles and amphibians we could find (this is called "herping"). My other most relevant job so far has been in the City of Philadelphia's Office of Transportation, Infrastructure, and Sustainability (oTIS), where I have worked both on transit and bicycle infrastructure analysis and outreach.

My Favorite Way to Make Fried Rice

I have gotten better at making fried rice as of recently. I'm not a fantastic cook, but can manage with day-old rice to make something lovely (it *has* to be day-old). Using my a big ol wok, first transform a few eggs into a slightly undercooked omelet, which then goes on a plate for later. With a clean wok, throw in onion, bell pepper, then frozen peas and carrots, or whatever veggies you desire; don't forget to use plenty of oil. Cook these on high heat, and once the veggies are sizzling, dump in the rice and break it up til the grains are mostly separated. When the rice has heated up a little, add the ingredients that make the rice turn that beautiful golden-brown color and taste amazing: some oyster sauce, MSG, salt, pepper, dark soy sauce, and Shaoxing wine if you have it. Mix thoroughly, and just after turning off the heat, stir in toasted sesame oil to make your stomach scream at you. Lastly, top with green onions and a squiggle of Sriracha. Voila, fuel for another night of coding.
